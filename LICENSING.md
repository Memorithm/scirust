## Provenance, integrity marking & licensing (disclosure)

> **Template — have counsel review and adapt before relying on it.** This clause
> discloses the integrity/provenance mechanisms present in SciRust so that their
> use is transparent and so that removal is a knowing act. Operational setup is
> in [docs/PROVENANCE_OPERATIONS.md](docs/PROVENANCE_OPERATIONS.md).

**1. Provenance marks.** Artifacts generated by SciRust and binaries built from
it may carry cryptographic provenance information — including a hash-based
(Lamport/Merkle) signature embedded in generated source, and functionally-neutral
execution-path markers — that identifies the originating engine and build. These
marks are **copyright-management information (CMI)**. You agree not to remove,
alter, obscure, or falsify them, and not to distribute copies from which they
have been stripped. (In the United States this conduct is separately actionable
under 17 U.S.C. § 1202; equivalent protections may apply elsewhere.)

**2. Numeric integrity.** The provenance marks are **output-neutral**: they do
not alter the numeric results SciRust computes, and the framework's bit-exact
numeric paths are protected by reproducibility tests. A watermark-free build is
available for reproducibility-sensitive use (build with the relevant marking
features disabled — the default configuration), so integrity marking never
compromises scientific reproducibility.

**3. Licensing enforcement.** Certain high-value capabilities (for example the
GPU acceleration module) may require a valid, signed license to run. Enforcement
is **graceful and non-destructive**: an unlicensed build declines to arm the
gated capability and returns a clear error — it never corrupts data, degrades
results, damages hardware, or silently alters output. Verification is performed
**entirely offline**; SciRust does not phone home, and license enforcement
transmits no telemetry or usage data. A license may optionally be bound to a
machine you designate; you may re-activate on a new machine by installing the
license file you were issued, without contacting the vendor.

**4. No covert data collection.** The mechanisms in §§1–3 collect and transmit no
personal data or workload content. Any per-licensee identifier exists only within
license metadata and provenance marks for the purpose of authenticity and leak
attribution, as disclosed here.
