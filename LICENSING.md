## Provenance, integrity marking & licensing — EU disclosure

**1. Provenance marks = electronic rights-management information (RMI).**
Artifacts generated by SciRust, and binaries built from it, may carry
cryptographic provenance information — a hash-based (Lamport/Merkle) signature
embedded in generated source, and functionally-neutral execution-path markers —
identifying the originating engine and build. This is **electronic
rights-management information** within the meaning of **Article 7 of Directive
2001/29/EC** (InfoSoc), transposed in France in the Intellectual Property Code
(CPI, art. L.331-11 et seq., with associated criminal sanctions). You must not
knowingly remove, alter, obscure or falsify it, nor distribute copies from which
it has been removed or altered, where you know or have reasonable grounds to know
that doing so induces, enables, facilitates or conceals an infringement.

**2. Your non-waivable software rights are preserved.** Nothing in this clause
restricts, and it shall not be read to restrict, the rights you hold under
**Directive 2009/24/EC** on the legal protection of computer programs that cannot
be excluded by contract — in particular the right to **observe, study and test**
the program to determine its underlying ideas and principles (Art. 5(3)) and the
right of **decompilation for interoperability** (Art. 6); any contractual term
contrary to these is null and void (Art. 8; in France, CPI art. L.122-6-1).
Sections 1 and 5 concern the integrity of provenance information and personal
data and are **without prejudice** to these mandatory exceptions.

**3. Numeric integrity & reproducibility.** The provenance marks are
**output-neutral**: they do not alter the numeric results SciRust computes, and
the bit-exact numeric paths are protected by reproducibility tests. A
**watermark-free build** is available (the default configuration, with marking
features disabled), so integrity marking never compromises scientific
reproducibility or your ability to independently verify results.

**4. Licensing enforcement — graceful, offline, proportionate.** Certain
high-value capabilities (e.g. the GPU acceleration module) may require a valid,
signed licence to run. Enforcement is **graceful and non-destructive**: an
unlicensed build declines to arm the gated capability and returns a clear error —
it never corrupts data, degrades results, damages hardware, or silently alters
output. Verification is performed **entirely offline**; SciRust does not phone
home and transmits no telemetry or usage data. A licence may optionally be bound
to a machine you designate; you may re-activate on another machine by installing
the licence file you were issued, without contacting the vendor. For any licensee
who is a **consumer**, this enforcement applies without prejudice to conformity
and remedy rights under **Directive (EU) 2019/770** (supply of digital content
and services) and to protection against unfair terms under **Directive
93/13/EEC** (France: Code de la consommation, art. L.212-1 and L.224-25-1 et
seq.); any term that would be unfair, or that would deprive a consumer of a
mandatory right, does not apply to that consumer.

**5. Data protection (GDPR).** The mechanisms in §§1–4 collect and transmit **no**
personal data or workload content; there is no telemetry and no phone-home. A
licence file contains the licensee's identity and licence id and — where you opt
into node-locking — a **pseudonymised, salted fingerprint** of a machine
identifier you supply (a SHA-256 value; the raw identifier is never stored),
processed under **Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)**. Where such data relates to an
identifiable natural person, the vendor processes it as controller on the lawful
